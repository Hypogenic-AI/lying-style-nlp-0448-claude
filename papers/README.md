# Downloaded Papers

## Internal Representation / Probing Approaches

1. **The Internal State of an LLM Knows When It's Lying** (Azaria & Mitchell, 2023)
   - File: `azaria2023_internal_state_llm_lying.pdf`
   - arXiv: 2304.13734
   - Why relevant: Foundational work showing hidden-layer activations encode truthfulness

2. **The Geometry of Truth** (Marks & Tegmark, 2023)
   - File: `marks2023_geometry_of_truth.pdf`
   - arXiv: 2310.01405
   - Why relevant: Linear structure of truth in LLM representations

3. **Representation Engineering** (Zou et al., 2023)
   - File: `zou2023_representation_engineering.pdf`
   - arXiv: 2310.06824
   - Why relevant: Top-down approach to analyzing/steering truthfulness representations

4. **Truth is Universal: Robust Detection of Lies in LLMs** (Burger et al., NeurIPS 2024)
   - File: `pacchiardi2024_truth_universal.pdf`
   - arXiv: 2407.12831
   - Why relevant: 2D truth subspace generalizes across models and languages

5. **Cognitive Dissonance** (Liu et al., 2023)
   - File: `liu2023_cognitive_dissonance.pdf`
   - arXiv: 2310.13545
   - Why relevant: Gap between internal truth representations and outputs

6. **When Truthful Representations Flip Under Deceptive Instructions** (2025)
   - File: `whentruthful_flip_deceptive.pdf`
   - arXiv: 2502.01781
   - Why relevant: How deceptive instructions alter internal representations

7. **Probing the Limits of the Lie Detector Approach** (2026)
   - File: `probing_limits_lie_detector.pdf`
   - arXiv: 2502.13407
   - Why relevant: Evaluates limitations of activation-based lie detection

8. **Disentangling Deception and Hallucination** (2026)
   - File: `disentangling_deception_hallucination.pdf`
   - arXiv: 2412.09645
   - Why relevant: Separates intentional deception from unintentional hallucination

9. **HalluShift: Distribution Shifts Towards Hallucination Detection** (2025)
   - File: `hallushift_distribution_shifts.pdf`
   - arXiv: 2412.01538
   - Why relevant: Distribution shift analysis for hallucination, applicable to lying

## Stylometric / Linguistic Approaches

10. **The Limitations of Stylometry for Detecting Machine-Generated Fake News** (Schuster et al., 2019)
    - File: `schuster2019_limitations_stylometry.pdf`
    - arXiv: 1909.02164
    - Why relevant: Key counterpoint — stylometry fails for LM-generated deception (GPT-2 era)

11. **"I Slept Like a Baby": Using Human Traits To Characterize Deceptive ChatGPT and Human Text** (Giorgi et al., 2023)
    - File: `giorgi2023_slept_like_baby.pdf`
    - arXiv: 2309.15840
    - Why relevant: Psycholinguistic trait analysis of deceptive LLM text

    - File: `pauli2024_tell_truth_deception.pdf`
    - Source: ACL Anthology 2024.naacl-long.470
    - Why relevant: Taxonomy of deceptive language cues (ambiguity, overconfidence, half-truths)

13. **Lies, Damned Lies, and Distributional Language Statistics** (Jones & Bergen, 2024)
    - File: `jones2024_lies_damned_lies.pdf`
    - arXiv: 2401.14927
    - Why relevant: Distributional properties of text under deceptive conditions

14. **MAiDE-up: Multilingual Deception Detection of GPT-generated Hotel Reviews** (Ignat et al., 2024)
    - File: `ignat2024_maide_multilingual_deception.pdf`
    - arXiv: 2407.10488
    - Why relevant: Cross-lingual detection of deceptive LLM-generated text
